Wind Forecasting

Sailing is a wind-dependent activity. Although we'd love to travel where the wind blows, we typically go to our port. So check out the wind forecast before you head to the port!

The National Weather Service (NWS) provides a variety of forecast tools for us to use. Start with the Big Picture. Wind is driven by pressure gradients. Look at the isobars (lines of constant pressure). Is the pressure constant across the West? Sad times, no wind.

Next, check out the NWS SAC Forecast Discussion NWS SAC Forecast Discussion for a plain-english overview of what's going on over the next few days.

Next, move to the Mesoscale Graphical Winds to see general wind speeds and directions. And, finally, the NWS SAC Hourly Forecast Note that the hourly prediction is typically only valid day-before and, even then, may be off.
