Commitment & Experience

Do I need experience to join?
  • No experience is required! We will teach you everything you need to learn to be a competitive sailor!

When are practices?
  • Practices are generally 3-4 hours every Saturday and Sunday.
  • In addition, we try to have weekday practices at the convenience of our members.

When are regattas?
  • Regattas are typically on the weekends and you can expect to be gone all day or all weekend depending on the location.

How do I get to practices and regattas?
  • We carpools to all of our practices and events.

Do I need to come to meetings?
  • Yes. Meetings have important info on regattas, practices, and activities.
  • To be an active team member make sure to come the weekly meeting every Thursday night at 9PM, in the MU Fielder Room.

I’m not sure if sailing’s for me.
  • Don’t worry! We give prospective members two free practices before they have to commit.

Gear

Am I expected to bring my own gear?
  • You are expected to bring your own gear. However, the team has a limited supply of extremely used foul weather gear that we loan to new sailors. We also have loaner life-jackets but they are not recommended for racing.
  • There are many places to find affordable sailing gear, but if you can’t buy actual sailing gear try finding substitutes! (Dry fit, surf booties, and gardening gloves are great substitutes!)

If I don’t have any sailing gear what do I need to bring to practice?
  • Bring a bathing suit, towel, water, snacks, and a change of clothes. Plan to get wet.

Financial Information

Is the team a charitable organization?
  • Yes. We are a 501©3 organization.

What are the member dues?
  • Undergraduates: $115 to Davis Sailing Team, $15 to Lake Washington Sailing Club.
  • Graduates: $25 to Davis Sailing Team, $15 to Lake Washington Sailing Club.

What are the regatta fees?
  • Each sailor has regatta fees included with club dues, excluding out of state Regattas such as Hawaii or the east coast.

Is financial aid available?
  • The team has provided financial aid for sailors to compete in certain events. Financial aid is on an application based process that is evaluated for each event.

Prospective Freshmen

What role can my sailing play in the admissions process?
  • Unfortunately, a desire to sail for UC Davis cannot assist you in the admissions process.

Can I get a scholarship for sailing?
  • No. Unfortunately our governing body, Inter-collegiate Sailing Association (ICSA) does not allow any sailor to receive a sailing related scholarship at any school.

How cool are you guys?
  • Extremely.

Sailing Aspects

What types of boats do we sail?
  • We sail the Collegiate Flying Junior (CFJ.), which is a 14-foot, 2 person dinghy.
  • Additionally, we own a single-crew, Olympic-class Laser.
Will I get to chose whether I get to skipper or crew?
  • Yes. We let our sailors chose which position they prefer. We encourage all sailors to be proficient in both positions. However, we encourage people to certain positions depending on their size, skill and enthusiasm.

How much do I need to know about racing?
  • Nothing! We will teach you everything that you need to know. That said, if you have race experience, that’s awesome!

Teams

How do you make varsity?
  • The varsity team is chosen by skill. But don’t get discouraged if you haven’t sailed before. Half our varsity learned in college and already have the skills to race at the varsity level.

What is the difference between JV and Varsity?
  • The JV and Varsity teams practice, travel and race together. The only difference between the team is in scoring and eligibility. There are both varsity only and JV only regattas the team attends.

What is the difference between a student organization and a club or varsity sport?
  • A student organization is student run, meaning we have no overseeing administrator. All our officers are students elected by our peers and are very aware of the students’ budget, time, commitment and needs. The officers also gain valuable leadership and resume experience which they would not receive on a varsity sport.
  • As student organization, we are not only able to attend regattas without university restrictions but also maintain our equipment more efficiently. We cannot have our funding cut and we can receive donations.

Team Leadership

Who is in charge of the team?
  • A group of six students are elected to be the officers of the team. It is a great leadership opportunity and looks great on a resume.

Do we have a coach?
  • Our head coach is also our captain, who is both an officer and a student. The captain is in charge of organizing our experienced volunteer coaches (from outside the team) and all skipper crew placements.
